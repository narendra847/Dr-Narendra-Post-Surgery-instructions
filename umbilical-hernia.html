Umbilical Hernia Repair

Your Post-Operative Care Instructions

What Was Done

A hernia is when tissue from inside your belly pushes through a weak spot in your belly button area. During surgery, we pushed this tissue back where it belongs and repaired the weak spot in your muscle wall. This was done through a small cut near your belly button.

Pain Management

Regular Pain Relief

  • Paracetamol: Take 2 tablets every 6 hours
  • Ibuprofen: Take 400mg every 8 hours with food
  • Continue for up to 1 week or until pain settles

Stronger Pain Medicine (If Needed)

Only use prescription pain medicine (opiates) if pain stops you from:

  • Walking
  • Getting out of bed
  • Coughing or taking deep breaths

Wound Care

You CAN

  • Shower normally - your wounds are sealed with medical glue
  • Let water run over the wounds
  • Pat dry gently with a clean towel

You CANNOT

  • Take baths for 4-6 weeks
  • Go swimming for 4-6 weeks
  • Use hot tubs or spas for 4-6 weeks

Lifting Restrictions - VERY IMPORTANT!

Do NOT lift more than 3kg for 6 weeks

3kg is the weight of a 3-litre jug of milk

This includes:

  • Heavy shopping bags
  • Children or pets
  • Heavy laundry baskets
  • Vacuum cleaners
  • Exercise weights

Driving

You can drive when ALL of these are true:

  • 1
    You have waited at least 1 week after surgery
  • 2
    You can comfortably wear a seatbelt without pain
  • 3
    You can make an emergency stop without pain
  • 4
    You are NOT taking prescription pain medicine (opiates)
  • 5
    You have checked with your insurance company

Warning Signs

Contact your local doctor if you notice:

  • Increasing redness around the wound
  • Swelling or puffiness around the wound
  • Increasing pain (pain getting worse, not better)
  • Fever or feeling unwell
  • Discharge or fluid from the wound

Activity and Recovery

What You Should Do

  • Walk regularly - start as soon as you feel able
  • Gradually increase your activity each day
  • Rest when you feel tired
  • Return to work when comfortable (usually 2-4 weeks depending on your job)

Follow-Up Appointment

You need to see me again in 6 weeks.

Please call my private clinic to book this appointment.

Take care of yourself and follow these instructions carefully.

If you have any concerns, don't hesitate to contact your doctor.
