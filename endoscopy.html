Endoscopy

Your Post-Procedure Care Instructions

Your Endoscopy Report

You should have received a report about your endoscopy procedure. This report contains important information about what was found during your examination.

Please read your endoscopy report carefully

The report will tell you what was found and any recommended next steps

What Was Done

An endoscopy is a procedure where a thin, flexible tube with a camera (endoscope) is passed through your mouth to examine your food pipe (oesophagus), stomach, and the first part of your small intestine. This allows us to see the inside of your digestive system and check for any problems.

First Few Hours After Procedure

  • Rest for the remainder of the day

    You may feel drowsy if you had sedation

  • Do NOT drive for 24 hours if you had sedation

    Arrange for someone to drive you home

  • Avoid important decisions or operating machinery for 24 hours

    Sedation can affect your judgment temporarily

  • No alcohol for 24 hours

Eating and Drinking

Drinking

  • You can drink room temperature liquids as soon as you feel ready (usually after 1 hour)
  • Wait until the numbness in your throat has gone before drinking hot drinks

Eating

  • You can eat normally once you feel comfortable and there are no dietary restrictions specified on your report
  • Start with light, easy-to-digest foods
  • Unless your report says otherwise - always check your report for specific instructions

Normal After Effects

These symptoms are normal and should settle within 24 hours:

  • Sore throat or feeling of something in your throat
  • Feeling bloated or having wind (from air used during the procedure)
  • Mild discomfort in your stomach
  • Feeling tired or drowsy (from sedation)

Warning Signs

Contact your doctor immediately if you experience:

  • Severe chest pain or abdominal pain
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Vomiting blood or vomit that looks like coffee grounds
  • Black, tarry stools (bowel motions)
  • Fever (temperature above 38Â°C)
  • Difficulty swallowing that gets worse
  • Increasing abdominal swelling

If Biopsies Were Taken

If tissue samples (biopsies) were taken during your endoscopy:

  • This is normal and routine - biopsies help us get more information
  • Results usually take 1-2 weeks to come back from the laboratory
  • You may notice a small amount of blood in your stools - this is normal
  • Your report will tell you if biopsies were taken

Follow-Up

Check your endoscopy report

Your report will tell you if you need a follow-up appointment and when.

If your report says to follow up in the rooms:

Please call my private rooms to make an appointment.

Return to Normal Activities

  • You can return to normal activities the next day
  • Most people return to work the next day
  • Resume your normal diet unless your report says otherwise
  • Continue your regular medications unless advised otherwise

Remember: Your endoscopy report contains important information specific to you.

Please read it carefully and follow any specific instructions it contains.
